Mountain Goat Steam Ale

Appearance
Slightly hazy yellow with a thick white head. Lots of carbonation bubbles.
Aroma
A nice collection of hops, sweet malt, tropical fruit and light citrus.
Taste
Crisp light malt, and mild bitterness. A little grassy and a very clean finish.
Mouth Feel
Fairly light body. Crisp, dry, smooth and refreshing.
Overall
An easy to drink, light beer. Quite refreshing so good for hot weather.

Little Creatures Bright Ale

Appearance
Clear light golden color. No head.
Aroma
Light citrus and hops.
Taste
Taste is similar to the aroma with a bit of citrus and malt sweetness.
Mouth Feel
Light body.
Overall
A light beer. Very easy to drink. Good for summer. More like a macro-brewed beer.

Coopers Pale Ale

Appearance
Pale golden color with bubbly head. Bottle fermentation leads to some residual yeast that can make it look more hazy.
Aroma
Light lemony herbal hop, some doughy bread and nutty yeast in the nose.
Taste
Hops drop a leveled bitterness and lemon / herbal flavor.
Mouth Feel
Nice and smooth. Yeast is in the back with a fading graininess as well as a slow drying palate.
Overall
Decent pale ale.

Knappstein Reserve Lager

Appearance
Pours a clear, pale yellow.
Aroma
Fruity in the nose, with some melon, grape, and light herbal notes.
Taste
The fruitiness emerges with suggestions of nectarine and lychee. Spicy, tingly noble hop character. Quite hoppy and playful, with complex herbal and floral notes.
Mouth Feel
Very smooth on the palate. Medium bodied, rounded and even.
Overall
Very German Export Lager-esque, but with a unique nod to a Sauvignon Blanc, no doubt thanks to Knappstein's use of Sauvin hops.

Grand Ridge Gippsland Gold

Appearance
Dark copper-brown colour with moderate fine carbonation.
Aroma
Malt and yeast dominate the nose, with aromas of caramel.
Taste
Tatse of malt with a bit of caramel, some fruit and trace of grain. Easy bitterness and a dry finish.
Mouth Feel
Medium to light body.
Overall
Well balanced, tasty English Pale Ale.

Peroni

Appearance
Very pale yellow color.
Aroma
Peppery and lemony hop, mild cooked veggies (faint cabbage) and a faint husky grain in the nose.
Taste
Bitter herb, cooked veggies and a slight metallic tone to start. Bone-dry palate with a graininess that is borderline astringent.
Mouth Feel
Smooth and crisp with a light body.
Overall
A plain euro lager. Uninteresting.

Trumer

Appearance
Bubbly golden straw hue with perfect clarity.
Aroma
Skunky, reminding of Euro Style Pilsners
Taste
Fresh cut hay, lemon zest and a faint biscuity maltiness.
Mouth Feel
Moderate body with a long drawn out crispness and solid bitterness.
Overall
A pretty remarkable German Pilsner. Brewed in Berkley, CA.

Hoegaarden

Appearance
Pale yellow and cloudy.
Aroma
Peppery, hints of lemon zest, with a mild horse aroma.
Taste
The brew is sweet. The yeast creates a pepper flavour that complements the light hop (lemony), sour flavour -- unique to this style.
Mouth Feel
The aftertaste dries, from the unmalted wheat, and a slight bitterness escapes at the very end.
Overall
The original White Ale, and grandfather to many a Summer-style white ale.

Asahi Super Dry

Appearance
Crystal clear dark straw colour. No head.
Aroma
Slight metal, grainy odor. Very hard to smell anything.
Taste
Lots of husky malt with metallic undertones from an astringent flavour, hops were quick and snappish with a sharp bitterness that went hand and hand with the husk flavour.
Mouth Feel
Super light and pretty dry with super high carbonation.
Overall
Clean, easy-drinking beer for thirst quenching. Not a sipper's beer.
